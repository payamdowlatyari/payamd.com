Watch and Review a Movie

Solaris (1972)

Director: Andrei Tarkovsky


A psychologist is sent to a station orbiting a distant planet in order to discover what has caused the crew to go insane.

Solaris is another masterpiece by Tarkovsky and one of the earliest Si-Fi/Drama movies full of artistic scenes and poetic moments. There aren't many directors like Tarkovsky that can capture the deepest human feelings in frames with so few or no words. Solaris, like the other movies by Tarkovsky, has a deep sense of mystery in almost all the scenes, in details, and overall flow of the story. The dialogues and monologues add more sophistication to the scenario and challenge its audience. It surprises you many times, makes you think about knowns and finally leaves you with many philosophical question and unexplored side of your psychee.
This film is about love, time, space, the universe, and many other ideas that vaguely exist in our minds. However, they have very different meanings for each person. The psychologist, who is initially going to discover the reason for the mysterious events that happened on that secret planet, like all other individuals who lived there, will eventually be stuck in an infinite loop of his darkest memories. Later, he found some explanations for the issue that was useful enough to free himself but his observations and analysis are not quite meaningful to others who have not been in the same situation.

Stalker (1979)

Director: Andrei Tarkovsky


A guide leads two men through an area known as the Zone to find a room that grants wishes.

This masterpiece is one of the finest movies of all time about the purpose of life and philosophy of human existence. Three men look for some meaning in a strange and dangerous part of the world. A writer and a professor without knowing each other join the third man, the Stalker, who seems like a loser but he knows something they don't. They follow him to find what they missed in life. Stalker is a mysterious man who spent his life and put a lot of effort into getting to that point and now he has become the guide for others. His wife and child are two other fascinating characters who look even stranger than Stalker himself. The conversation between these three men in the journey makes up the story and artistic shots by Tarkovsky creat a poetic and meaningful stoyline.

Three Colors: Blue (1993)

Director: Krzysztof Kieslowski


A woman struggles to find a way to live her life after the death of her husband and child.

Blue is the color of sadness and comfort. The movie starts with the creation of a disaster in a woman's life. When she wakes up in hospital she realizes she lost her husband and child. This is the beginning of a tough experience with lots of struggles in her thoughts. Julie who was the wife of a famous musician must cope with reality and accept what she has faced in life. Julie tries to free herself from memories and everything related to the past. However, the past incidents in life won't leave her alone such that she finds out about her husband's mistress. The movie ends with a great act of forgiveness and understanding. Although her husband cheated on her, once she realizes that woman is pregnant, Juile only thinks about the newborn baby, who is about to join the journey of life. She donates her husband's house to the mistress to free herself from all the bad feelings to start the next chapter fresh.

Three Colors: White (1994)

Director: Krzysztof Kieslowski


After his wife divorces him, a Polish immigrant plots to get even with her.

I think the openning and closing of this film is the best between all three the way that the author/director surprises the audience with unusual and uncanny shots. The film starts with showing a suitcase in the airport, then shifts to a Paris divorce court where a Polish barber is talking with the judge similar to a scene in Blue. After a series of events after the divorce, the guy who was an immigrant in Paris with no money, comes back to Poland his home country for the sake of survival. Sometime later, the camera shows the guy, who now became a successful person in France, visits his ex-wife in prison and sees her through her cell window. As she gestures to him that she wants him back, the guy starts to cry like a lost child who just found his mother.
